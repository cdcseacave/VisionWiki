## TL;DR

**Stub page — not yet ingested.** Lyra 1 is the direct precedent to [[shen2026_lyra2|Lyra 2.0]]. It distills the implicit 3D knowledge in a video diffusion model into an explicit 3DGS representation via a **self-distillation** framework: the standard RGB decoder is augmented with a 3DGS decoder, supervised by the RGB decoder's output, so the 3DGS decoder can be trained purely on synthetic data generated by the video diffusion model itself — no multi-view capture required. At inference the model synthesizes 3D scenes from a text prompt or single image; the framework extends to dynamic 3D from monocular video.

## Why this stub exists

Lyra 2.0 (April 2026) cites Lyra 1 as `[2]` and inherits from it:

1. **The "generative reconstruction" paradigm** — generate camera-controlled video + lift to 3D via feed-forward reconstruction — is framed as established precedent in Lyra 2.0 §1 Introduction.
2. **Fine-tuning a feed-forward reconstructor on generated (rather than real) data** to close the domain gap from generative artifacts: Lyra 2.0 §4.4 explicitly says *"Similar to Lyra [2], this improves robustness to artifacts commonly present in generative data."* This is the specific mechanism Lyra 2.0 inherits and does not re-claim as novel; it belongs to Lyra 1.

## Relation to [[shen2026_lyra2|Lyra 2.0]]

- **What Lyra 2.0 keeps**: the generative-reconstruction loop (generate video → feed-forward lift); the pattern of fine-tuning the reconstructor on generated scenes.
- **What Lyra 2.0 adds**: long-horizon consistency mechanisms (per-frame 3D cache + geometry-aware retrieval + canonical-coord correspondence injection), self-augmented drift-mitigation training, DMD distillation with retained self-aug, downsampled Gaussian DPT head, hierarchical sparse-grid mesh extraction.
- **What Lyra 2.0 *contrasts against* Lyra 1**: Lyra 2.0's anti-forgetting design is explicitly anti-"global 3D memory" (see [[information-routing-vs-3d-rendering-memory]]); Lyra 1 belongs to the global-3D-memory family. The concept page treats Lyra 1 as a rendering-memory representative.
